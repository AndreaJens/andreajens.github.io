She looked at the shelf, pretending not to do that, then touched the small sequence of dots in a wide swipe. The label, those small extrusions on the paper-thin plastic, spelled the words “Tomato Soup”. In reality, what was in front of her, was a can of peas, of the same size and shape as what usually occupied that spot. She was seeing that clearly, from the other side of her one-directional blindfold. Yet, what if she had been blind for real and not pretending to be blind, for the safety of all people around her? Would have she taken that can, bought it, and then found out about the difference once she got home? A cold shiver down her spine. Visually impaired people did have it hard, especially when the personnel at the supermarket was that level of idiotic. She grabbed a real can of soup, still pretending not to be able to see, then she carefully navigated the corridors, using a cane. It had been weird to have to learn to move that way. Chai’s sight was almost perfect. Yet, she had been forced to watch the world through a black silk screen since when she was not even ten years old. It was ten o’clock in the night, and there was no other customer around. Chai used to go that late to shop because it reduced the chances to stumble into a bunch of people and accidentally lose her blindfold. That happened once, and the result hadn’t been nice. Furthermore, she had learned to know all the late night employees, sometimes even having small chats with them. That one was new, though. Never seen her at Molco, maybe a student in need of some money and taking on odd jobs?

Chai decided to ask her later, just for some idle chat. She laid her items on the counter, one after another, with a monotonous rhythm. She stopped for an instant. The cashier was looking at her, almost as awoken from her state of lethargy. Chai feigned indifference, kept on emptying her bag. She had to learn to never behave as if she could see. All her life, hiding behind her blindfold. Even during her most prized, intimate moments. She could never open her eyes while being kissed, while having the time of her life. “Print, thanks.”

Chai proffered her hand, as she was used to do. Payment via fingerprint was extremely comfortable in her situation, but of course needed a little bit of external help to work, especially for someone who couldn’t legally see.
